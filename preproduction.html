Writing the Screenplay

The first step of most movies is writing a script. If there isn't a story, what do you intend to film? Screenplays are written in the present tense and feature a lot of dialogue. There are points in a screenplay where important actions are described, but oftentimes, the director will add their own actions while filming. Most screenplays are structured in a way that one page is equivalent to about one minute of screen time. Therefore, scripts tend to be between 90 and 180 pages (equal to about 1h 30m and 3h, respectively).

Storyboarding and Creating a Shot List

After the screenplay is done, you will want to visualize how scenes will actually be filmed. Storyboarding is the next step, where you will draw boxed pictures of how you want the camera placed, what the actors will be doing, and what the set will look like. Storyboards resemble comic book panels quite a bit, and function a similar purpose.

Linking with storyboards, shot lists are like written storyboards. You will go through every shot you plan on filming and write down everything you can about it. You'll make tables to show the shot number, the location, the shot type (closeup, establishing shot, master shot, etc.), the camera angle, camera movement, and a description of what you are filming. A shot list will make it much easier to set everything up and break shots apart to be filmed in a better order (for example, you may shoot all of your actors at one point in time, and all scenes without actors at another time.

Finding a Cast, a Crew, and Locations

You need to have people in order to make a movie. You can put out a casting call or just ask friends. You'll need actors to film and crew to operate the behind-the-scenes equipment. You'll also need to find locations to film at. If you have a small budget, you probably won't want to spend it all on building sets. You'll have to drive around and find places that suit your script. Make sure you get permission to shoot your movie at the locations, though.

Building Props and a Wardrobe

Chances are, you won't have every prop and costume already present when you go about filming. You'll probably have to hunt down items for your movie or you might even have to make them yourself. You definitely want to have everything ready before you start filming. You might also want to consider having duplicates of some items.
